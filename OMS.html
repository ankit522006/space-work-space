Orbital Maneuvering System (OMS)

The Orbital Maneuvering System (OMS) consisted of two aft-mounted AJ10-190 engines and the associated propellant tanks . The AK10-90 Engines used monomethylhydrazine (MMH) oxidized by dinitrogen tetroxicle (N204) . The OMS engines were ised after main engine cut-off (MECO) for orbital insertion .Throughout the flight they were used for orbit changes , as well as the de-orbit burn prior ro re-entry . Each OMS engine produced 27,080 N of thrust and the entire system could provide 305m/s of velocity change .
